Where Can You Go Hand-Gliding?

Experience the thrill of soaring like a bird in some of the most breathtaking locations around the world! Hand-gliding enthusiasts can find incredible spots for flying, whether you're seeking serene coastal winds or adrenaline-pumping mountain launches. Here are a few popular destinations: Coastal Cliffs: Glide over stunning seaside landscapes at destinations like Torrey Pines in California or the cliffs of Cornwall in England. Perfect for smooth rides and picturesque views. Mountain Launches: Take off from majestic peaks like Rio de Janeiro's Pedra Bonita or the Swiss Alps for heart-racing flights with unparalleled scenery. Open Countryside: Explore flatland flying in places like the Great Plains of the USA or Australia's scenic countryside, where thermals provide excellent lift. Tropical Havens: Enjoy warm winds and vibrant views in places like Hawaii, Bali, or the Dominican Republic. Whether you're a seasoned pilot or a first-time flyer, there’s a perfect spot waiting for you. Don't forget to check with local clubs and schools for the best times and safety guidelines. Eligibity for Hang-Gliding

Minors under 18 years will need a signed consent form from a parent or guardian. Weight Limits: For safety reasons, participants must weigh between [Minimum Weight] kg/lbs and [Maximum Weight] kg/lbs. Physical Fitness: Hang-gliding requires basic physical fitness. Participants should be able to jog a short distance and follow instructions clearly. Those with serious medical conditions should consult a doctor before participating. Health Conditions: Individuals with heart conditions, recent surgeries, or severe joint issues may not be eligible. Pregnant individuals are advised not to participate. Training Requirement: First-time flyers must complete a mandatory pre-flight training session with a certified instructor. Weather Suitability: Flights may be rescheduled or canceled in unsuitable weather conditions for your safety. Clothing and Gear: Participants should wear comfortable, weather-appropriate clothing and closed-toe shoes. All safety equipment will be provided. Alcohol and Drugs: Participants must not be under the influence of alcohol or drugs during the activity.
