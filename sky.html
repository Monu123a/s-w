Where is Sky Diving done?
Skydiving is done most frequently in India at specific locations that offer certified services, scenic views, and safety standards. Here are the top places: 1. Mysore, Karnataka Why Popular: Mysore is renowned for its well-organized skydiving experiences, with professional operators offering tandem and static-line jumps. Highlights: Panoramic views of Chamundi Hills and lush green landscapes. Best Time: October to March. 2. Aamby Valley, Maharashtra Why Popular: Located near Lonavala, this luxurious spot is perfect for adventure enthusiasts seeking tandem skydives. Highlights: Stunning views of the Western Ghats and pristine surroundings. Best Time: October to February. 3. Deesa, Gujarat Why Popular: One of India’s first skydiving spots, Deesa is known for its professional setup and training programs. Highlights: Expansive landscapes and tranquil water reservoirs. Best Time: October to March. 4. Aligarh, Uttar Pradesh Why Popular: Close to Delhi NCR, Aligarh offers tandem jumps and skydiving training programs, making it a convenient option for adventure seekers in North India. Highlights: A great starting point for first-time skydivers. Best Time: October to February. 5. Pondicherry, Tamil Nadu Why Popular: Coastal skies make skydiving in Pondicherry a unique experience, with breathtaking views of the sea and beaches. Highlights: A mix of adventure and serene natural beauty. Best Time: November to March.
Eligibilty for Sky Diving
Age Requirements Minimum Age: Most operators require participants to be at least 18 years old. Parental Consent: In some cases, participants aged 16–18 years may be allowed with written parental or guardian consent. 2. Weight Limit Most operators set a maximum weight limit for skydivers: Typically 90–100 kg (200–220 lbs) for tandem jumps. Heavier individuals may need to pay extra fees or get special approval. 3. Physical Fitness You do not need to be an athlete, but general good health is essential. People with the following conditions are usually not allowed: Heart problems or conditions. Severe back or neck injuries. Epilepsy or seizures. Recent major surgeries. Pregnancy. It's advisable to consult a doctor if you have pre-existing health concerns. 4. Medical Declaration Participants may need to sign a medical declaration form before the jump. Some operators might ask for a doctor’s certificate confirming fitness for skydiving. 5. Alcohol and Drug Policy You must be sober and free from any drugs or alcohol for at least 24 hours before your jump.
