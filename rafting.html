Where is River Rafting done?

Rishikesh, Uttarakhand Known as the rafting capital of India, Rishikesh offers rafting on the Ganges River. The rapids range from Grade I to Grade IV, suitable for beginners and experts alike. The Shivpuri to Rishikesh stretch is the most famous. Manali, Himachal Pradesh The Beas River near Manali provides an exciting rafting experience with Grade II and III rapids. The journey offers beautiful views of the Kullu Valley. Ladakh, Jammu and Kashmir Rafting on the Zanskar River and Indus River is an unforgettable experience. The Zanskar’s "Grand Canyon" offers Grade III and IV rapids, surrounded by rugged, barren landscapes. Teesta River, Sikkim and Darjeeling The Teesta and Rangeet Rivers offer rafting opportunities in the Northeast, with a mix of thrilling rapids and calm stretches amidst lush greenery and hills. Coorg, Karnataka The Barapole River in Coorg provides rafting with exciting rapids set against the Western Ghats' stunning backdrop. It’s ideal for thrill-seekers in South India.

Eligibity for River Rafting

Age Most rafting operators require participants to be at least 12 years old for moderate rapids (Class III) and 16 years old for more intense rapids (Class IV-V). Some family-friendly rafting trips on mild rapids (Class I-II) allow younger children, typically 6 years or older. 2. Physical Health Participants should be in good physical health, as rafting can be physically demanding. Certain medical conditions, like heart problems, severe back issues, or pregnancy, may disqualify someone for safety reasons. 3. Swimming Ability While not always mandatory, being able to swim is highly recommended. Even with a life jacket, strong swimming skills are helpful in case of capsizing or other water-related challenges. 4. Weight Limit Some operators have weight limits (e.g., 250-300 lbs), depending on the equipment and safety considerations. 5. Skill Level For intense rapids (Class IV-V), prior rafting experience or training might be required. 6. Weather and Water Conditions During high water seasons, operators might tighten eligibility criteria for safety.
